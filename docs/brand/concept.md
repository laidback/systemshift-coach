## 1. Brand Concept

### Positioning Statement
> **systemshift.coach** is the coaching practice of Lukas Ciszewski — systemic coach and IT professional with 20+ years in tech. He works with IT leaders, engineers stepping into leadership, and anyone navigating complexity in technology organisations.

This is not generic coaching with an IT veneer. Lukas is the product. The brand lives at the intersection of deep systemic coaching methodology and lived experience inside tech organisations.

### Brand Name: systemshift
"Systemshift" works on two levels:
- **Technical:** Systems thinking is native language in IT. A "shift" is a change, a context switch, a transformation.
- **Coaching:** Systemic coaching is about shifting how someone sees and relates to their system — their team, organisation, relationships, and self.

The name is precise, memorable, and communicates expertise without explaining itself.

### Tone of Voice
- **Direct** — no corporate fluff, no coaching clichés ("unlock your potential")
- **Peer-level** — speaks *with* IT people, not at them
- **Calm confidence** — not inspirational-poster energy. Quiet authority.
- **Honest** — acknowledges difficulty without dramatising it
- **Sparse** — IT people value signal-to-noise ratio. So does this brand.

**Avoid:** "transform your life", "journey", "empower", "holistic", "passion"
**Use:** "clarity", "system", "complexity", "what's actually happening", "your next move"

### Target Audience

| Segment | Situation | Core need |
|---|---|---|
| Tech Lead / Engineering Manager | First leadership role, technically strong, people-challenges surprising | Learn to lead without losing technical identity |
| Senior IC at crossroads | 10+ years in, questioning direction or value | Clarity on what's next and why |
| IT professional under pressure | Burnout-adjacent, overloaded, system feels out of control | Space to think, identify levers |
| Tech founder / CTO | Organisational complexity scaling faster than leadership capacity | Systems lens on their own organisation |

### Value Proposition
> *You don't need more advice. You need better questions — from someone who understands your world.*

Lukas brings two things most coaches don't have simultaneously:
1. **Systemic coaching methodology** (ISTOB C25 — structured, evidence-based)
2. **20+ years inside tech organisations** — he knows what a sprint feels like from the inside, what it means to be the only one in the room who understands the architecture, and what happens when technical excellence stops being enough

### Differentiators
| Generic Executive Coach | systemshift.coach |
|---|---|
| Learns your context from scratch each session | Already fluent in tech organisational dynamics |
| Applies universal frameworks | Understands why frameworks break down in engineering cultures |
| Corporate polish | Tech-native directness |
| "Journey" language | Systems language |
| Broad audience | Specific niche — depth over breadth |

## 4. CoachBot Concept — built by TechBot, for Lukas only. Not client-facing. Never visible to clients.

### Design
- **UI:** Minimal, dark theme
- **Access:** Lukas only — private tool, not exposed externally
- **Tone:** Functional, no fluff. Built for someone who thinks in systems.

### Core Functions

**A. Session Preparation**
- Before each client session: prompt Lukas with last session summary, client's stated focus, open threads
- Suggest 2–3 opening questions based on client context
- Flag anything time-sensitive (goal deadlines the client mentioned, follow-ups promised)

**B. Reflection Prompts (Post-Session)**
- After each session: structured debrief questions for Lukas
  - "What moment had the most energy in this session?"
  - "What did you notice that you didn't name out loud?"
  - "What would you do differently?"
- Store reflections in client file for pattern recognition over time

**C. Resource Library**
- Searchable library of systemic tools, questions, and frameworks
- Lukas adds resources; CoachBot surfaces relevant ones based on client situation description
- Categories: leadership transitions, team dynamics, burnout/recovery, career decisions, systemic questioning techniques

**D. Client Follow-Up Drafts**
- After session: draft a brief follow-up message for Lukas to review/send
  - Session summary (what we explored, not what was said)
  - Any agreements or next steps the client mentioned
  - Invitation to next session
- Lukas reviews and sends — CoachBot never sends directly

**E. Admin / Practice Management**
- Upcoming session reminders with client prep notes
- Invoice drafts (Lexoffice integration)
- Monthly practice summary: sessions held, topics, pipeline

### Data Principles
- All client data stays local / encrypted
- CoachBot has access to Lukas's notes — never to raw session recordings
- Client names anonymised in any cross-client analysis

### Integration Points
- Calendar (session scheduling awareness)
- Notes files (per-client markdown or structured DB)
- Message tool (draft follow-up → Lukas approves → sends)
- Eventually: Lexoffice API for invoicing

## 5. Starter Checklist — Launch systemshift.coach in Germany

### Legal & Admin
- [ ] **1. Check Hilti employment contract** — locate Nebentätigkeitsklausel, submit written Nebentätigkeitsgenehmigung request. Do this *before* any paid client.
- [ ] **2. Gewerbeanmeldung** — register Nebengewerbe at Gewerbeamt Landsberg am Lech (~20–40 €). Coaching is generally "gewerblich" in DE (not freiberuflich).
- [ ] **3. Kleinunternehmerregelung** — elect §19 UStG status at first tax registration (no VAT on invoices until 22.000 €/year revenue). Simpler, better for starting out.
- [ ] **4. Business liability insurance** — Betriebshaftpflicht for coaches: ~100–150 €/year (e.g. Hiscox, ERGO, Allianz).
- [ ] **5. Separate bank account** — N26 Business or Kontist (both free, good for Selbstständige). Keeps business cashflow clean from day one.
